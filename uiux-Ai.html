Project Overview

Project outline / Clients’ names / Project goals / Major deliverables

Objective:

Through Introductor, the AI Medical Referral Software, insurance agents can access health information to help policyholders choose the ideal hospital and surgeon for surgery. As a result, insurance agents can develop strong and reliable relationships with policyholders to increase the likelihood of continued success in the insurance agent business.

Overview:

About 90 per cent of policyholders call an insurance agent for accurate and up-to-date information about their health coverage levels after a possible surgery. Likewise, more than half of policyholders asked insurance agents for advice on more alternative information about choosing a hospital or surgery centre. Still, about half of the policyholders were reluctant to advise because almost no policyholders had the medical knowledge to plan the surgery. In addition, people judge the quality of hospitals by different criteria. Advising policyholders on hospital or surgery can lead insurance agents into ethical dilemmas.

To address this question, we conducted interviews with insurance agents to observe pain points and scenarios. Insurance agents sincerely want to help clients choose the right medical speciality. They often seek guidance from colleagues and browse official government and hospital websites for the latest data and insights on choosing hospitals and specialists. However, it is difficult for the best professionals to schedule consultations, and most of the health information they can find online is often fragmented and incomplete. This dazzled them and made it difficult to find the data they were looking for. The entire process is time-consuming and inefficient.

Thus, this software is designed to assist insurance agents to help their customer service in finding hospitals and surgeons. To that end, the team consulted the perspectives of healthcare professionals to establish an objective and unbiased standard for evaluating the quality of software developed by hospitals and surgeons. Likewise, data is visualized as infographics that insurance agents can easily understand and use as a tool to explain the strengths and weaknesses of hospitals and surgeons.

Insurance agents say the software provides understandable judgment to help their clients make thoughtful decisions. Likewise, during doctor consultations, patients also use data from the software to discuss the outcome of possible surgery.

Starting the Design

Include problems, solutions, flowcharts, wireframes to understand the beginning of a design

Problem:

Design a simple and direct user interface that communicates useful healthcare information to insurance agents, enabling insurance agents to help their clients gain objective and unbiased insights when choosing the ideal surgeon and hospital. By doing so, policyholders can make informed decisions.

Almost insurance agents mentioned that the best solution is to help policyholders make informed decisions during planned surgery, rather than providing a list of surgeons. With this in mind, we interviewed 9 insurance agents and 7 policyholders to gain more constructive insights into the decision-making and planning process for surgery. The observations are as follows:

  • 6 out of 16 respondents preferred choosing the best doctors
  • Over 31% of respondents cited hospital location as their top concern
  • 3 respondents said hospital and surgery costs were their number one priority
  • 1 in 8 respondents highlighted medical equipment, nurse-patient ratios, and surgical performance metrics as important to them

Depending on the results, policyholders have different concerns during the planning stage. In addition, insurance agents say their clients are not of the same generation, so they use different communication tools to conduct business.

Solutions:

To help insurance agents (primary users) provide policyholders with excellent customer service (finding ideal surgery centres and surgeons), the software is equipped with the following solutions to meet policyholders' needs.

Users Want Alternative Search Methods:

As mentioned earlier, people have different priorities in the criteria for choosing a hospital and a surgeon. To solve this problem, users can find the information they want through other search methods. Users can start by finding an authoritative doctor in the field, or set search criteria based on location.

Users Want A Network of Physicians:

Respondents expressed difficulty in arranging a consultation with an authoritative physician, even a disciple or partner of an authoritative physician. The software analyzes the authoritative physician’s social graph based on her/his doctoral dissertation writing, surgical collaboration, graduation school, etc. Providing the authoritative physician’s network map, the policyholder can make an appointment with her/his disciple or cooperative doctor.

Users Want All Information in One Place:

Almost medical-related data is scattered across different websites, and users want an aggregated presentation that includes everything a policyholder needs during a planned surgery.

Data Needs to Be Organized and Defined:

To help users easily access and understand the meaning of the data, data was sorted based on the priority to comprehend the story the data is telling.

Users Need Data to Visualize:

To communicate medically relevant information clearly and effectively, we use statistical graphics, charts, and infographics to convey quantitative information visually.

Multiple Ways to Deliver Results:

As mentioned earlier, insurance agents serve different generations, some are used to reading articles and statistics on paper, but others receive documents via email and instant messaging. To meet the needs of all policyholders, the software allows users to choose to send the output via email, instant messaging, or print the results on paper.

Flowchart:

To let the team understand the sequence of steps and decisions required during the use of this product, we created a flowchart.

Wireframe:

Wireframes are designed to find how users prefer to search and what information is needed to choose a surgeon. After many tests with users, the first step of the program is to choose a search method based on the user's needs.

Refining the design

UI Design:

To enable users to have a more cohesive user experience, experience a consistent design language and facilitate the smooth completion of the task at hand, we created a design system. Design systems have gradually increased the consistency and speed of software development.

To develop the design system, firstly, rules and principles were established. Secondly, a colour palette was finalized to determine the primary colour and secondary colour. Then, typographic scale, icon libraries, and other styling properties such as padding and margins are standardized to eliminate inconsistencies and improve maintenance and scalability by reducing design and technical debt.

Colour:

Since blue and green are often associated with trust, knowledge, and professionalism, we used blue and green to convince users that the product provides credibility and expert advice to those picking hospitals and specialists.

Typography:

To allow users to read text effortlessly, Calibri is adopted because of its readability and legibility in web and mobile user interfaces
